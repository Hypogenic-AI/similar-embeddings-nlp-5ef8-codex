# Downloaded Papers

1. Word Translation Without Parallel Data (Conneau et al., 2017)
- File: papers/2017_conneau_word_translation_without_parallel_data.pdf
- Source: arXiv:1710.04087
- Why relevant: Core unsupervised cross-lingual word embedding alignment method (MUSE).

2. A robust self-learning method for fully unsupervised cross-lingual mappings of word embeddings (Artetxe et al., 2018)
- File: papers/2018_artetxe_robust_self_learning.pdf
- Source: ACL 2018 (P18-1073)
- Why relevant: Strong baseline for unsupervised cross-lingual mapping (VecMap).

3. Cross-lingual alignment of contextual word embeddings, with applications to zero-shot dependency parsing (Schuster et al., 2019)
- File: papers/2019_schuster_cross_lingual_alignment_contextual_embeddings.pdf
- Source: arXiv:1902.09492
- Why relevant: Aligns contextual embeddings across languages, relevant to sense-related similarity.

4. Context-Aware Cross-Lingual Mapping (Aldarmaki and Diab, 2019)
- File: papers/2019_aldarmaki_context_aware_cross_lingual_mapping.pdf
- Source: arXiv:1903.03243
- Why relevant: Studies context-aware mapping of word embeddings, bridging sense variation.

5. Cross-lingual Language Model Pretraining (Lample and Conneau, 2019)
- File: papers/2019_lample_conneau_xlm.pdf
- Source: arXiv:1901.07291

6. Unsupervised Cross-lingual Representation Learning at Scale (Conneau et al., 2019)
- File: papers/2019_conneau_xlm_r.pdf
- Source: arXiv:1911.02116
